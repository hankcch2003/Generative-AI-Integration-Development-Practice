問題 2：把『I'm feeling under the weather today.』翻譯成道地的台灣繁體中文。
回答：There are a few ways to translate "I'm feeling under the weather today" into Taiwanese Mandarin, depending on the nuance you want to convey:

**Formal:**

* **「今天有點不舒服。」 (Jīntiān yǒu yīdiòbù fù chéng.)** - This is a direct translation that emphasizes being slightly unwell.
* **「我今天身體不太舒服。」 (Wǒ jīntiān shēn bǐ bù tài shūhé.)** - This translates to "I'm not feeling well physically today."

**Informal:**

* **「今天不舒服。」 (Jīntiān bù fáng.)** -  This is a more colloquial way of saying "feeling under the weather" and is used when referring to general unwellness.


**Other Considerations:**

* **Context:** Knowing the specific situation can help determine the most appropriate translation. If talking about a cold or flu, using terms like "感冒 (gǎn mà)" or "發燒 (fā shāo)" might be more fitting. 
* **Tone:** Taiwanese Mandarin often uses euphemisms when referring to illness. For example, instead of saying you're "sick", you could say "不舒服" or "身體不太舒服".


Ultimately, the best translation will depend on the specific situation and your desired level of formality.
